Standard analytics

Standard

The daily operating picture. Available on the Overview and Menu tabs, scoped to Today or the last 7 days, with a per-branch filter for multi-branch owners. Numbers always compare against a like-for-like previous period so a manager instantly knows “better or worse than usual.”

Daybook headline Standard

The hero figure: completed revenue, total orders, average order value and completed count, each with an up/down delta pill.

HowRevenue = sum of COMPLETED order totals. AOV = revenue ÷ completed orders. Delta compares to the same weekday last week (Today) or the immediately preceding window (multi-day).
WhyThe 5-second answer to “how are we doing right now?” The like-for-like delta strips out normal week-rhythm so a quiet Monday isn’t mistaken for a slump.

Sparkline Standard

A mini bar chart beside the headline — orders by hour (Today) or revenue by day (multi-day), with the peak bar highlighted.

HowToday → busiest-hours counts; multi-day → daily revenue series.
WhyShows the shape of the day or week at a glance — where the rush lands and whether momentum is building or fading.

Insight strip Standard

Up to three plain-language callouts in the same voice as the weekly email (e.g. “2pm is your busiest hour”, “Latte is 24% of revenue”).

HowDerived from busiest hours + best-seller revenue share. Pro adds weak-converting item & at-risk-regulars callouts.
WhyTranslates charts into sentences a manager can act on without reading a single axis.

Order-status breakdown Standard

A segmented bar splitting the period’s orders into completed, ready, in-progress, pending and cancelled/declined.

HowCounts grouped by OrderStatus over the window.
WhyAn operational health check — a fat pending slice means the line is backing up; a growing cancelled slice means orders are slipping through.

By time of day Standard

Orders and revenue bucketed into Morning, Midday, Afternoon, Evening and Late night, peak daypart flagged.

HowOrders grouped by daypart in the café’s timezone; all five buckets always shown, zero-filled.
WhyDrives staffing and promo timing — push a happy-hour offer into the soft afternoon, staff up before the evening peak.

Trend chart Standard

A revenue/orders area chart over the selected multi-day range, toggleable between the two metrics.

HowPer-day completed revenue and order count, gap-filled so every day in range has a point.
WhyShows direction over time — is last week’s promo still lifting sales, or did it fade after day two?

Best sellers Standard

Ranked top items by quantity sold and revenue, on the Menu tab.

HowSum of quantity and line totals per menu item over the window, top 8 shown.
WhyThe backbone of menu decisions — what to feature, keep stocked, and never run out of.

Pro analytics

Pro

The diagnostic layer. Pro unlocks longer ranges (30 / 90 days & custom), the Team and Customers tabs, and deeper cards across every tab. Where Standard tells you what happened, Pro explains why — and what to do about it. Each route is gated by Entitlements.requirePro(); Standard cafés see an upgrade prompt instead.

Extended date ranges Pro

30-day, 90-day and fully custom date windows, on top of Standard’s Today / 7 days.

WhyShort windows show noise; longer windows show trends — seasonality, month-over-month growth, the real effect of a price change.

Item conversion Pro

Per item: how often it’s viewed vs how often it’s ordered — a view→order rate, with weak performers flagged “low”.

HowRate = quantity ordered ÷ menu-item views (from analytics_events). Sorted worst-first to surface wasted attention.
WhyFinds items that get looked at but don’t sell — usually a photo, price or description problem, not a demand problem. High-traffic, low-conversion items are the fastest revenue wins.

Conversion funnel Pro

The customer journey: menu view → add to cart → checkout started → order placed, with step-to-step % and one overall conversion number.

HowEvent stages count distinct browsing sessions; the order stage counts placed orders. Overall % = orders ÷ menu views.
WhyPinpoints where customers drop off. A big fall at checkout points to friction (payment, delivery options) — a very different fix than a fall at “add to cart”.

Ordered together Pro

Market-basket pairs — the items most frequently bought in the same order (e.g. Croissant × Flat White).

HowCounts item pairs co-occurring across orders; only pairs seen in ≥2 orders are shown.
WhyFuels combos, bundles and upsells — pair proven companions into a meal deal, or prompt “add a …?” at checkout.

Weekly rhythm Pro

A demand curve per weekday showing expected orders by hour, averaged over the last 4 weeks, with each day’s peak hour marked.

HowOrders grouped by weekday × hour over the trailing window, divided by weeks-in-window to get an expected-per-hour figure.
WhyA forward-looking staffing & prep plan — know that Saturday spikes at 11am before it happens, not after.

Staff performance Pro

A team leaderboard: orders completed, accepted, declined and average accept time per staff member.

HowAggregated from the order event log by actor over the window.
WhyTurns service speed into something coachable and fair — recognise the fast acceptors, spot who’s overloaded or sitting on tickets.

Kitchen timing Pro

Average seconds per fulfillment stage — to accept, prep, handoff — plus to-ready and total, with the slowest step flagged as the bottleneck.

HowFrom order-event timestamps; each stage averages only orders that reached both of its ends, over a stated sample size.
WhyIsolates the one stage costing the most time. If “to accept” is the bottleneck it’s a front-of-house staffing issue; if “prep” is, it’s a kitchen one.

Customers — regulars & at-risk Pro

Two lists: top regulars (your most loyal) and going quiet (regulars who’ve stopped showing up).

HowRegular = ≥3 orders. At-risk = a former regular with no order in the last 21 days. Top 8 of each.
WhyPowers loyalty and win-back — reward the regulars, and reach a lapsing customer while a nudge still works rather than after they’re gone.

Customer base Pro

Retention KPIs: repeat rate, avg orders per customer, new this month, active in the last 30 days, and repeat-order share (first-time vs returning).

HowRepeat rate = repeat customers ÷ total. Repeat-order share = (orders − customers) ÷ orders, since each customer’s first order is their own.
WhyThe single best gauge of business durability. Revenue from regulars is cheaper and steadier than constantly buying new customers — this shows whether the base is compounding.

Benchmark Pro

Your café vs the anonymised Serva median — average order value and accept time, shown as a percentile.

HowComputed across all cafés over 30 days; only released when ≥5 comparable cafés exist, so no single café is identifiable.
WhyAdds outside context — a 90-second accept time only means something next to what comparable cafés achieve.
Implementation note. The Benchmark card is built and gated server-side but currently commented out in the dashboard UI pending a rework (small-sample UX, per-café volume floor, like-for-like cohort). Everything else above is live.
